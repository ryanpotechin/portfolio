Consumer Product · MS Capstone · Northeastern University

Disposal Defense — Garbage Disposal Safety Insert

Problem

Individuals with garbage disposals frequently drop silverware and other non-food items into the drain, risking costly damage and injury. Existing products block water flow, require complex installation, or fail to stop the most common items.

Engineering Process

Ran a full structured PD process: 14-person user survey, 4-patent IP landscape search, competitive benchmarking of 4 existing products, 12 concept sketches across 5 categories, two rounds of Pugh matrix down-selection (12→6→1), and a 10-person preference study with audio-recorded interviews. The winning concept — “Double Ramp” — uses brachistochrone (cycloid) curves on both ramps to maximize water/food flow while deflecting rigid items like silverware. Both parts injection-moldable in Santoprene with press-fit pin-hole assembly.

Deliverables

Full engineering drawing package (Top Ramp, Bottom Ramp, assembly drawing), costed BOM ($3.00/unit; $30K tooling), physical FDM prototype tested in a real sink, and a 54-slide final presentation. All drawings are GD&T-compliant with 1° draft angles and uniform wall thickness for injection molding readiness.

Medical App · Bioengineering Capstone Lead · Northeastern University

MedCheckMe — Smartphone Medical Diagnostic App

Overview

Project lead on a 6-person capstone team sponsored by the Social Ventures Foundation building a smartphone-based medical screening app for underserved communities in Haiti and Costa Rica. Goal: all diagnostic checkups under $1 per patient.

Technical Scope

Four diagnostic modules: (1) Cardiology — PPG extracted from smartphone camera video, processed in MATLAB with band-pass filtering and pulse wave analysis to estimate heart rate and blood pressure; (2) Audiology — pure tone audiometry via earbuds, 250–8000 Hz; (3) Optometry — Tumbling E visual acuity and Ishihara color blindness plates; (4) Dermatology — dermoscopic skin lesion classification using ML. Hardware trade study selected Google Pixel 5. Backend: PostgreSQL on Google Cloud with encrypted, de-identified patient records.

My Role

Defined product requirements, led technical reviews across all four modules, developed the PPG signal processing pipeline in MATLAB, and managed all project documentation and design decision records across a full academic year.

Sports Technology · Product Development · Northeastern University

SkateSharp — Portable Skate Sharpening Device

Problem

Skate sharpening has remained largely unchanged for decades — high long-term costs ($10–25 per session), inconsistency across technicians, time lost traveling to pro shops, and no solution for blade issues mid-game. A portable, consistent, and affordable device could address a real gap in the $500M market.

Engineering Approach

Defined core value propositions (portability, consistency via auto-alignment, affordability, versatility). Conducted competitor analysis against BladeMaster, ProSharp, and Sparx Hockey. Developed user personas for coaches, athletes, and parents. Identified a 10–15% target market share ($50–75M revenue potential). Applied Stage-Gate framework with defined exit criteria at each gate, and validated assumptions through user interviews. Refined initial scope to focus first-generation device on hockey skates before expanding to figure skating.

Healthcare Tech · MS Product Development · Northeastern University

RapidAlign — ER Resource Management Platform

Problem

Emergency departments are strained by overcrowding, misplaced equipment, and delayed bed assignments — driven by systems operating in silos. Existing RTLS and bed management software lack coordination during patient surges, leading to delayed care, staff burnout, and documentation errors.

Solution

RapidAlign: a unified ER platform integrating real-time bed tracking with automated availability notifications, RTLS-based equipment tracking, predictive analytics for patient surges, and role-specific task assignment dashboards. Designed for HIPAA compliance and rapid hospital adoption.

Validation & Market

Interviewed 10 emergency department workers. 70% reported daily delays from resource silos. Target outcomes: 20% reduction in arrival-to-triage time, 30% faster resource location, 25% fewer disposition decision delays. TAM: $10B; Target Market: $1B (large metropolitan hospitals).
